Port of Yokohama

Sofia Kanto (関東)

The Port of Yokohama is operated by the Port and Harbor Bureau of the City of Yokohama in Japan. It opens onto Tokyo Bay.

Osanbashi Pier is the main international passenger pier at the Port of Yokohama, located in Naka Ward, Yokohama, Japan. Osanbashi Pier is a scenic spot for many Japanese dramas.

The rooftop of the International Passenger Ship Center, with its wide deck stretching out to the sea, offers a view of the passenger ships docking.
The most important thing you can’t miss here is the night view of Yokohama. With the orange-red sun gradually sinking from the Ferris wheel, the night is getting low, but it awakens the skyline of Yokohama, which is beautiful.

The Yokohama Red Brick Warehouse is a historical building that is used as a complex that includes a shopping mall, banquet hall, and event venues.

The Red Brick Warehouse was first a major trading and customs base in Yokohama, but now it has been transformed into a historic cultural and creative attraction, with many cultural and creative businesses and restaurants now located there.

Yamashita Park is a public park in Naka Ward, Yokohama, Japan, famous for its waterfront views of the Port of Yokohama.

You can see the beautiful scenery of Yokohama, and Yamashita Park, which is about one kilometer long along the coastline, is the first waterfront park in Japan, and at night there is a glittering Ferris wheel and the Yokohama Crossing Bridge.

Yamashita Park’s harbor-side “Nippon Post Ship Hikawa Maru” was a medical ship during World War II and a high-class banquet venue after the war.
Today, it is a museum of Japanese postal ship history with early historical items on display and a view of the Ferris wheel and the Red Brick Warehouse across the water.

Yokohama is about 40 minutes from Shinjuku, Tokyo. If you are tired of Tokyo or want to experience a different city by the sea in the suburbs, Yokohama is indeed a good choice.

Visiting Information

✔JR: Take the JR Express from Shinjuku, Tokyo for about 30 minutes, and the fare is 550 yen.

✔Tokyu Railway: Take the Tokyu Toyoko Line Limited Express from Shibuya, Tokyo, and the fare is 270 yen for a 30-minute ride.

✔Keikyu Railway: From Tokyo Asakusa, take the Toei Asakusa Line for a direct connection to the Keikyu Main Line, which takes you to Yokohama in 40 minutes and costs 580 yen.
