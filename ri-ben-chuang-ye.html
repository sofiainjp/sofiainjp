日本創業 I (Start up I)

Sofia 日本創業

想在日本待超過3個月時間，最重要的莫過於「簽證」。日本政府在2005年時制定了法規以利外國經營者長住日本，就是所謂的「投資經營簽證」，到了安倍政權時，更進一步放寬外國人在日本創業時的限制，也把「投資經營簽證」改名為現在的「經營管理簽證」。「經營管理簽證」就是在日本創立公司，進行經營和管理行為，就可以申請的日本中長期滯留資格的簽證。

當我人還在台灣的時候，就開始上網搜尋資料、了解申請經營管理簽證的條件。下面將之前找的資訊整理並記錄一下。

  1. 公司經營規模：新公司的投資金額必須在500萬日幣以上，或者雇用兩名以上的全職員工。因為雇用員工除了要支付每個月的薪水外，還必須負擔員工部分社會福利(年金、保險等)的費用，所以大多數的人都選擇投資至少500萬日幣（最少金額），而初期有租借辦公室、請稅理士結帳、公司設立申請等花費開銷。重要的事是，申請經營管理簽證的本人，做的必須是「經營管理的工作」。
  2. 事業計畫：申請簽證時，必須向入國管理局提交公司的事業計畫書，詳述公司的業務內容、發展方向，更重要的是必須說服日本政府，事業計劃書所提出的公司業務內容具備可行性及持續性，也就是要讓日本政府了解你有長久經營的打算及可行的商業模式。
  3. 事務所要求：有實體的獨立辦公室，共享辦公室或只是租用一個公司地址或虛擬辦公室是無法通過申請的。
  4. 經營權要求：申請人必須擁有公司的實質經營權。

「經營管理簽證」希望申請人有實質的經營管理的行為，能夠長久穩定經營，為日本社會帶來稅收及就業機會。很多有錢人，認為在日本買房收租金做投資，就可以拿到經營管理簽證，並進一步移民日本，但事實上是，如果只是買不動產收租金進行投資，那麼就不涉及經營管理這樣的行為，不符合取得經營管理簽證的資格。

上述都是網路上找得到的茲料。但實際狀況是怎麼樣呢？

首先是公司經營規模部分，雖然資料上提到資本額有500萬日幣就可以，但實際上，要考量你是要單獨過來還是會有家人一起過來，想當然，資本額越高是越好的，畢竟要在日本生活沒有想像中的容易，有很多稅金、保險料金要繳交呢！

再來是事業計畫書，要把自己未來的事業好好做好一番規畫及審視，用中文完成後在請專業的翻譯人員協助翻成日文。千萬不要以為像是做一份單純的報告，這份計畫書其實是協助自己了解事業是否能在日本存活下來的重要依據，如果過去曾有創業經驗，當然很容易撰寫，但如果是菜鳥新手，有很多的細節必須要考量、思考。所有的開立公司成本（租借辦公室、網路費、水電費、電話費、聘請專業人員像的費用、產品的成本等等），以及收入是否能不赤字？這些都是透過事業計畫書可以幫助自己了解的。當然，也可以花錢請行政書士協助你，但是還是要自己先規劃過一遍才能更清楚細節不是嗎？畢竟未來要成為公司的老闆，不能不知道公司的財務及願景啊！！

至於到底要怎麼租辦公室？要如何匯款到日本？這個雞生蛋蛋生雞的問題.....

If you want to stay in Japan for more than three months, the most important thing is the "visa". In 2005, the Japanese government enacted regulations to facilitate foreign businessmen to stay in Japan for a long period of time, the so-called "investment and management visa", and under the Abe regime, further relaxed the restrictions on foreigners starting a business in Japan and renamed the "investment and management visa" to the current "management visa". The "business management visa" is a visa for mid- to long-term stay in Japan that can be applied for by establishing a company in Japan and carrying out business and management activities.


When I was still in Taiwan, I started to search for information on the Internet and learn about the requirements for applying for a business management visa. The following is a summary of the information I found.

  1. Company size: The new company must invest at least 5 million yen or employ two or more full-time employees. Most people choose to invest at least 5 million yen (the minimum amount) because they have to pay the monthly salary as well as the social benefits (annuity, insurance, etc.) of the employees. The important thing is that the person applying for a business management visa must be doing "business management work".
  2. Business plan: When applying for a visa, you must submit a business plan to the Immigration Bureau, detailing your company's business content and development direction, and more importantly, you must convince the Japanese government that the business plan is feasible and sustainable, i.e., that you have a long-term business plan and a viable business model.
  3. Office requirements: a physical office, a shared office or a rented office address or a virtual office is not acceptable.
  4. Operating right requirement: Applicant must own the actual operating right of the company.

The "Business Management Visa" expects the applicant to have substantial business management activities and to be able to run a stable business for a long time, bringing tax revenue and employment opportunities to the Japanese society. Many rich people think that if they buy a house in Japan and collect rent as an investment, they can get a business management visa and further immigrate to Japan, but the truth is that if they only buy real estate and collect rent as an investment, then they are not involved in business management and are not eligible for a business management visa.

All of the above is the information available on the Internet. But what is the actual situation?

First of all, the company's scale of operation, although it is mentioned in the information that a capital of 5 million yen is fine, but in reality, it is necessary to consider whether you want to come alone or with your family, of course, the higher the capital is the better, after all, it is not as easy as you think to live in Japan, there are many taxes and insurance materials to pay!

Then there is the business plan, which is a good plan and review of your future business, completed in Chinese and then translated into Japanese with the help of a professional translator. Don't think of it as a mere report. This plan is actually an important basis to help you understand whether your business can survive in Japan.What are all the costs of setting up a business (office rental, internet, utilities, phone, hiring professional staff, cost of products, etc.), and can the income be generated without a deficit? These are all things that a business plan can help you understand. Of course, you can also pay an executive clerk to help you, but you still have to plan it yourself first to get a better idea of the details, right? After all, if you want to be the boss of a company in the future, you can't be unaware of the company's finances and vision!

How do I rent an office? How do I send money to Japan? This chicken begets the egg begets the chicken question .....
