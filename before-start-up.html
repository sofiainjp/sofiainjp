Before Start up  in Japan

Sofia Startup in Japan (日本創業)

Many people like to travel to Japan. For Taiwanese, the frequency of visiting Japan may be once, twice or more a year, but have you ever thought about living in Japan?

I love Japan so much that I often arrange time to see cherry blossoms and maples in Japan during my busy work schedule.

I had imagined that I would live in Japan instead of just traveling, enjoying and experiencing the local life, and feeling the change of seasons, but in reality, it was almost a "dream or fantasy". I never thought that I could really move to Japan in 2022, before the global epidemic has completely subsided.

The most common ways to live in Japan for more than three months are to seek a job in Japan or to marry a Japanese person. If you are still young and have some savings, you can first go to a Japanese language school to improve your language skills and make friends, and then consider finding a job in Japan. Both of these options seem to be suitable for young people.

I am not young anymore, so we choose to start a business in Japan.

It is not easy to start a business in Japan. First, imagine what you have to give up. It may be a stable and respectable job, a sheltered house, a car, and many other physical or abstract things. After thinking about what you might need to let go of and accepting the reality of opportunity costs, then start thinking about how to live in Japan, or to put it plainly, how to make a living.

It's easy to travel to Japan when you enjoy your vacation because you only need to pay for it with your salary. However, if you want to live in Japan, having savings can really help you survive, but having savings alone will not allow you to live in Japan for a long time because the most important thing is the "visa" (work visa, family stay visa, business management visa, etc.). Can you really make money? The above two items are still slightly under your control, but there are many, many things that you cannot control in the future.

After experiencing the idea that "many things are beyond my control", I seem to be able to "appreciate every opportunity and encounter" and live a more grateful and appreciative life.

How do I start a business in Japan? How do I start in a foreign country?
