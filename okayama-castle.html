Okayama Castle

Sofia Chugoku Region (中国地方)

Okayama Castle is a Japanese castle in the city of Okayama. In stark contrast to the white "Egret Castle" of neighboring Himeji, Okayama Castle has a black exterior, earning it the nickname Crow Castle or "castle of the black bird". (The black castle of Matsumoto in Nagano is also known as "Crow Castle", but it is karasu-jō in Japanese.) 

The entrance to Tenshukaku is on the first basement floor (B1) of Tenshukaku. After purchasing a ticket, you can take the elevator from the right passage. The way to visit Tenshukaku is to take the elevator to the top floor and then go down one level at a time.

The elevator goes up to the 6th floor, where visitors can see the surrounding area of Okayama Castle. The western side of Okayama Castle faces the "Ujinko Road" and the "Joshita" station, and the southern side of Okayama City Library and Okayama Prefectural Office.

The stairs between floors are not as narrow and steep as those of the original Tenshukaku, but rather wide and gentle, and it is easy to descend from the 6th floor to the 5th floor.

When you look out of the window at the 5th floor, you can still enjoy a good view of the building, especially the shiny "Golden shachi" with gold leaf on the roof.

The nearby Okayama Koraku-en is also a must-see!  It is one of the Three Great Gardens of Japan, along with Kenroku-en and Kairaku-en. Korakuen was built in 1700 by Ikeda Tsunamasa, lord of Okayama. The garden reached its modern form in 1863.

After being severely damaged by the bombing of the Second World War, the government restored the park to its original state according to the paintings and designs of the Edo period. It is managed as a historical and cultural asset and will be passed on to future generations.

If you are interested in the historical figures of Japan's Warring States period, you can experience the pride of the Warring States daimyo and the impact of ancient Japanese wars on the rise and fall of generations in Okayama Castle. And the relaxation of Okayama Koraku-en can also be arranged in the same day trip! After the visit, you will feel a sense of physical and mental relaxation.
