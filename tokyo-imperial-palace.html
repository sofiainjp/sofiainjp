Tokyo Imperial Palace

Sofia Kanto (関東)

The Tokyo Imperial Palace is the main residence of the Emperor of Japan.The vast area includes historical buildings and seasonal flowers, and the interior is divided into three sections: the “East Imperial Palace”, the “Outer Imperial Palace”, and the “Inner Imperial Palace”, which requires a reservation, all at no extra charge.

The “Inside the Imperial Palace” area is accessible to the general public with prior reservation. The safest thing to do is to make a reservation on the Internet.The Tokyo Imperial Palace generally conducted twice a day in the morning and in the afternoon.

Directions for walk-in registration
・Bring a valid form of identification outside the Kikyo-mon gate of the Imperial Palace.
・Numbered tickets will be distributed one hour prior to the starting time of the tour. Take one ticket per person. Following the entry at the Kikyo-mon gate, you will be asked to fill in an application form.

Directions for prior registration(Online Application)
・Applications are accepted from 5:00 am on the first day of the month preceding the tour date.
・To apply, visit the website click on “Application for Visit,” select the date of your visit and fill in the required information. Register the members’ list within 7 days from the date of your application.
・Application should be completed no later than four days prior to the tour date.
・Upon completion of the application, a permit notice will be issued via the online application system.
・Once the application has been accepted, no changes may be made to the date and time of the tour, the list of participants, except reducing the number of participants.

Tour route: (about 2.2 km, one hour on foot, paved road)
Kikyo-mon gate (entry) => Someikan (visitor’s rest house) => Fujimi Yagura (“Mt.Fuji View” Keep) => Imperial Household Agency => East courtyard of the Imperial Palace =>Seimon-tetsubashi bridge (walk across back) => East courtyard of the Imperial Palace => Yamashita-dori street => Imperial Household Agency => Kikyo-mon gate (exit)

※Both preregistered visitors and those who have made walk-in registrations will participate together in the guided tour. As a general rule, visitors will not be permitted to turn back midway through the tour.

No tours are given on the following days and times:

・Sundays
・Mondays
・National Holidays (except when a holiday falls on a Saturday)
・Afternoons from July 21 to August 31
・From December 28 to January 4
・Any day or time of the day when Imperial Court functions are scheduled or other unavoidable circumstances occur.

Point of Interest in KOKYOGAIEN NATIONAL GARDENS ( Chidori-ga-fuchi Moat )

The shape of moat is said to resemble the Chidori(Plover bird). This moat used to connect to the Hanzo-bori moat, but in 1901, it was divided when a new road was constructed across the moat.

If you come to the Imperial Palace during the cherry blossom season, don’t miss the moat “Chidori-no-Yu” on the northwest side of the Imperial Palace, a famous cherry blossom viewing spot in Tokyo.
