The castle is regarded as the finest surviving example of prototypical Japanese castle architecture, Â and is frequently known as "White Heron Castle" because of its brilliant white exterior and supposed resemblance to a bird taking flight.

Himeji Castle is very large, covering an area of about 23 hectares, about five times the size of Tokyo Dome. About half of the area has been renovated as a park, and anyone can enter the park for free. The other area, centered around castle tower, requires a fee to enter the city.

Himeji Castle tour focuses on the interior of castle tower, where you can climb to the uppermost floor. There are many information about the history of Himeji Castle, and the miniature model shows the street scene in the past.

Himeji Castle is also a famous cherry blossom viewing spot. When you come to Kansai during the cherry blossom season, Himeji Castle is definitely not to be missed.
