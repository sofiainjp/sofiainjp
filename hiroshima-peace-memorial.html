Hiroshima Peace Memorial

On August 6, 1945, the U.S. Army bombed Hiroshima with an atomic bomb, and almost all of the buildings near the center of the blast were razed to the ground, except for this building, which barely stood and did not completely collapse. The atomic bomb dome was preserved as a memorial of the incident and is part of the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park.

This building was first completed in 1910 as the “Hiroshima Prefectural Products Exhibition Hall” and was renamed the “Hiroshima Prefectural Products Exhibition Hall” in 1921 and the “Hiroshima Prefectural Industry Award Hall” in 1933, making it an important exhibition venue in Hiroshima at that time.

Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum

The Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum is the largest museum in Hiroshima City related to the atomic bombing. The museum details the tragic events of August 6, 1945, and displays artifacts left behind after the atomic bombing so that people can empathize with the victims of Hiroshima.

The power of the atomic bomb was not limited to the many people who died on the spot, but also to the many people who suffered from the effects of the blast, heat injuries, and nuclear radiation. There are many sad stories in the museum, and although I was mentally prepared before visiting the museum, I still feel very strongly after visiting the museum, which makes people cherish the days of world peace.

Travel Information

From the JR Hiroshima Station

✔By Bus / About 20 minutes
•Take №24 Hiroshima Bus for Yoshijima from B-8 at the south exit of Hiroshima Station, and get off at “Heiwa kinen koen”.

✔By sightseeing loop bus “Hiroshima Meipuru~pu”/ About 17 minutes
•Take a “Hiroshima meipuru~pu” at the Shinkansen-guchi exit of Hiroshima Station, and get off at “Heiwa kinen koen”. Both the orange line and green line stops at “Heiwa kinen koen”.

✔By Streetcar / About 25 minutes
•Take a streetcar for Hiroshima Port , and get off at “Fukuro-machi”.
•Take a streetcar for Eba or Miyajima-, and get off at “Genbaku Domu Mae”.
